What are the Effects of E. Coli?

CDC.gov says, “Shiga-toxin producing E. coli is abbreviated as STEC. Symptoms of infection with this germ include watery or bloody diarrhea, fever, abdominal cramps, nausea, and vomiting. The illness can be mild to severe.” This explains the effects of E. Coli, the bacteria produces a toxin, which begins to infect the body and start the symptoms. But sometimes the symptoms come after 2 weeks or in between the two weeks.

What are the Symptoms

NHS Inform says, “Symptoms include diarrhea, stomach cramps and occasionally fever. About half of people with the infection will have bloody diarrhea. People usually notice symptoms 3 to 4 days after they have been infected. But symptoms can start any time between 1 and 14 days afterwards.” This explains the effects that happen to a human body when they are infected with E. Coli. This also says that the symptoms can be noticeable in the first 3-4 days, but can start anytime in two weeks and maybe after.
